# Competitor: Insomnia Cookies

**Type:** National chain (late-night focused bakery)
**Founded:** 2002, Philadelphia, PA
**Locations:** ~300+ stores in US, targeting 600+ by 2030
**Annual Revenue:** ~$750M (as of 2025)
**AUV per location:** ~$850,000
**Investors:** Verlinvest & Mistral Equity Partners

---

## What They Do Well

### 1. Late-Night Positioning
Insomnia created the late-night cookie category — open until 3 AM. This is their core differentiator. They own the "2 AM cookie craving" moment entirely. This drives a loyal college-town customer base and creates genuine brand identity around the hours.

### 2. Clean Unit Economics
$850K AUV with ~15% operating profit margin. Strong financial model that attracts franchise investors. The business is built for scale with a repeatable, proven format.

### 3. Omnichannel Strategy
Delivery (direct + DoorDash/UberEats), in-store pickup, and catering all work together. Their app is well-built for reorder and tracking. Multiple revenue streams per location.

### 4. Brand Consistency
Pink branding, late-night aesthetic, consistent menu feel across all locations. The brand feels cohesive even at scale — not generic, but not trying to be everything to everyone.

### 5. Growth Discipline
Backed by private equity but growing measured — not reckless expansion. Targeting higher-density urban markets and international. They're playing the long game.

---

## Where They Could Improve

### 1. Generic Brand Voice
No real personality beyond the hours. The brand doesn't feel warm or community-driven — it's transactional. No local flavor at any location. If you've seen one Insomnia, you've seen them all.

### 2. Limited Menu Innovation
Same core cookies for years. No seasonal drops that create urgency or excitement. The menu is reliable but not exciting. No flavor of the week, no limited drops, no campaign moments.

### 3. No Local Market Customization
Zero ability to flex on local tastes. A Fort Worth Insomnia looks identical to a Philadelphia one. No acknowledgment of regional cookie preferences (Texas loves butter, chocolate, simple things).

### 4. Corporate Brand Feel
Backed by PE, run by a growth team — it shows. The brand is polished but soulless. You won't see the owner of an Insomnia baking cookies or talking about their grandma's recipe.

### 5. Minimal Community Connection
No local events, no sponsorships, no farmers markets, no wedding presence. They take orders but don't build relationships. A caterer at heart but acts like a fast food chain.

---

## How Jonesy's Can Incorporate This

| Insomnia Tactic | Jonesy's Implementation |
|-----------------|------------------------|
| Late-night positioning | Jonesy's already mobile — could do "late-night delivery" for college/high-school events. Market the 10 PM cookie run |
| Strong unit economics | Track AUV per event. Build pricing that shows profit per dozen rather than just revenue |
| Omnichannel | Get on DoorDash + UberEats + direct website ordering. Minimize friction from "I want cookies" to "I get cookies" |
| Catering focus | Insomnia does this poorly. Jonesy's mobile model is actually better for events — lean into this harder |
| Brand consistency | Develop a consistent visual language: pink boxes aren't Jonesy's, but consistent branded packaging, labels, stickers for events |

---

## Jonesy's Advantage Over Insomnia

Insomnia is corporate. Jonesy's is local. Thurman can say "I baked these cookies yesterday" — the CEO of Insomnia can't. That's the moat. Lean into local authenticity, community events, and the neighbor-who-bakes narrative that a chain literally cannot replicate.

---

## Threat Level for Jonesy's

**Low.** Insomnia is a national chain playing a different game (volume, late-night, franchise scale). They have no real local presence in Corinth/Springtown area. Jonesy's real competition is other local bakers and the big delivery brands (DoorDash orders from national chains). Focus on local brand loyalty, not competing on geography with Insomnia.
