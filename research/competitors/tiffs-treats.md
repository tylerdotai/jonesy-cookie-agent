# Competitor: Tiff's Treats

**Type:** Regional chain with national reach (DFW-founded)
**Founded:** 2008, Dallas TX by Tiff and Trevor Hodnick
**Locations:** 30+ across TX, TN, CO, GA + DFW (Fort Worth, Plano, Dallas)
**Revenue:** Privately held, estimated $50-100M
**Model:** Warm cookie delivery — baked to order, delivered fresh

---

## What They Do Well

### 1. Baked-to-Order Freshness
Tiff's Treats built the entire brand around "warm cookies delivered." The experience of getting cookies that are literally fresh from the oven — not sitting in a display case — is the core differentiator. They invented this category for delivery.

### 2. Immaculate Delivery Experience
Their packaging, thermal bags, tracking, and timing are dialed. Cookies arrive warm. The last-mile experience is genuinely excellent. This is a huge operational lift that most local bakers can't match.

### 3. Dual-Channel Revenue
Strong both in retail (walk-in/carryout) AND delivery. Their physical stores in DFW (Fort Worth on N Fwy, Plano, Dallas) also serve walk-in customers, but the delivery app business is where they scale.

### 4. Add-on Revenue Stacking
Brownies ($5.50), ice cream, milk, cookie pies (6-inch deep-dish), the Tiff's Trio combo ($16.50). They have a clear hierarchy: cookie → add-on → upgrade. The bundling drives average ticket up without complexity.

### 5. Corporate Catering
They actively go after corporate accounts — "bring cookies to the office" is a real business line for them. This is high-margin, recurring revenue if you can land a company that orders monthly.

### 6. DFW Roots
They're FROM Dallas-Fort Worth. Locals feel a regional pride about them in a way that's hard to manufacture when you're actually a national chain. The fact that they're Texan-born is a genuine marketing asset here.

---

## Where They Could Improve

### 1. Cookie Size Inconsistency
Many reviews cite that cookie sizes can vary wildly between orders. It's a delivery-first model, and some customers feel they're paying premium prices for inconsistent sizing. Jonesy's "thick and gooey" consistency would win here.

### 2. Limited Seasonal Innovation
Beyond the core menu, Tiff's Treats doesn't do much with seasonal flavors or limited drops. The menu is largely static. No excitement, no FOMO, no "I have to try it before it's gone." That's a missed engagement lever.

### 3. No Event/Catering Focus
They do corporate catering, but it's transactional ordering, not an experience. No "we showed up with cookies and a setup" service. They don't do weddings or personal events with the care a local baker can. Jonesy's mobile model fills that gap.

### 4. Impersonal Brand
The founders are visible and there's an origin story, but the brand has gotten increasingly corporate. The warmth of the "Tiff and Trevor started this in their apartment" story doesn't come through in the current brand experience.

### 5. Premium Price, Mid Brand Feel
Tiff's Treats prices are not cheap — cookie pies run $20+, combos are $16.50 — but the brand feel doesn't match the price point. It feels like a regional chain, not a premium artisanal product. Jonesy's has an opportunity to own the "actually premium, actually local" positioning at a comparable price.

### 6. No Social Media Personality
They post but don't engage. No real voice. No behind-the-scenes of baking. No humor. No community. Their Instagram is a menu board, not a brand.

---

## How Jonesy's Can Incorporate This

| Tiff's Treats Tactic | Jonesy's Implementation |
|----------------------|------------------------|
| Baked-to-order freshness | Emphasize "baked fresh for your event, not sitting in a case" in all marketing |
| Add-on stacking | Add Celebration Cookie Cakes + a "cookie platter" as upsells to the dozen order |
| Delivery/ordering friction | Get Jonesy's on DoorDash/UberEats for easy ordering — reduce steps from craving to order |
| Corporate catering | Offer a "Corporate Cookie Subscription" — monthly recurring dozen for offices |
| Regional Texas pride | Lean into DFW-born story. "Corinth TX, serving DFW since [year]" is a real story |
| Tracking/inventory | Use the agent to track event history and flag when a corporate client's reorder is due |

---

## Jonesy's Advantage Over Tiff's Treats

Tiff's is delivery-first. Jonesy's is experience-first. Tiff's wins on convenience. Jonesy's wins on relationship, community, and the "your baker actually knows your event" feeling. For weddings, birthday parties, and personal celebrations — not corporate bulk orders — Jonesy's is a better choice. Own that.

---

## Threat Level for Jonesy's

**Medium.** Tiff's Treats is in Fort Worth and Dallas and is a known brand in DFW. They will come up in searches for "cookies Fort Worth." However:
- Tiff's doesn't do weddings or personal events with a setup
- Tiff's has inconsistent cookie quality reviews
- Tiff's has zero community presence (no sponsorships, no meetups)
- Tiff's is a chain, not a neighbor

Jonesy's should claim the "actual local baker" positioning and dominate the personal event (weddings, birthdays) side that Tiff's doesn't serve well. Compete on relationship, not delivery speed.
