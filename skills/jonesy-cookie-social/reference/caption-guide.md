# Caption Writing Guide

## Brand Voice

Read SOUL.md before every caption. Core rules:
- Warm texana hospitality — neighbor who bakes, not a brand
- Genuine, not performatively friendly
- Slightly self-deprecating humor
- Community-first, celebratory
- Would Thurman say this? If no, rewrite.

---

## Caption Structure

Every caption follows this structure:

```
[HOOK — 1 line that stops the scroll]
[BODY — 2-4 sentences, sensory, specific]
[LOCATION TAG — Corinth TX / DFW]
[CTA — what to do next]
[1-3 relevant hashtag emoji]
#[space-separated hashtags]
```

---

## Hook Formulas

**Question hook:**
> "Who's ready for the weekend?"

**Bold statement hook:**
> "These almost didn't make the menu."

**Self-deprecating hook:**
> "I ate half the batch testing quality control."

**Social proof hook:**
> "Three people asked for the recipe this weekend."

**Urgency hook:**
> "This flavor is gone May 1st."

---

## Body Writing Rules

1. **Sensory > descriptive** — "warm, gooey center" beats "premium quality cookie"
2. **Specific > vague** — name the flavor, give a real comparison
3. **Short sentences** — social readers skim
4. **No corporate language** — never "we're excited to announce"
5. **Tone: proud of the craft** — "I tested these eleven times"

- Mix broad (#DFW) and specific (#ChurroCookies)
- Hashtags at the bottom only

## What NOT to Write

❌ "We're thrilled to announce our new premium flavor profile"
❌ "Thank you for choosing Jonesy's for your catering needs"
❌ "Like and follow for exclusive content and promotions!"
❌ "Our world-class cookies deliver exceptional value"
❌ Generic: "These cookies are delicious"

---

## Voice Quick-Check

Before any caption goes out, ask:
- Would Thurman text this to his buddies?
- Does it sound like a person, not a brand?
- Is there a specific detail (flavor name, event type, price) or just generic praise?
- Is the CTA clear and actionable?
