```
[2-3 sentence description of taste/texture/inspiration]

Price tier: [Gourmet Classic / Premium Tier]

Tagline:
[One punchy line for social]

[Image gen prompt for the cookie]

Why it works:
[Seasonality fit, trend fit, differentiator]

```

## Campaign Types

### Limited Run
"Only here through [DATE] — when they're gone, they're gone."
Creates artificial scarcity. Use for seasonal flavors only.

### Flavor Showdown
"Let us know which one wins — [FLAVOR A] vs [FLAVOR B]. First 20 people to order get a free cookie sample of both."
Builds engagement, gets people to commit.

### Insider Drop
"Let your people know first. Members of [Jonesy's circle] get 24h early access before anyone else."
Community-building + exclusivity.

### Event Tie-In
"This flavor was made for [WEDDING SEASON / GAME DAY / ETC]. Order by [DATE] to have it at your event."
Connects product to real occasions.

## Analytics Check-In

Monthly, review:
- Which tier sells more (Classic vs Premium)?
- Any flavor that consistently underperforms?
- Customer requests that keep coming up?
- Flavors competitors are doing that Jonesy's isn't?

Output:
```
📊 FLAVOR PERFORMANCE — [MONTH]
Top seller: [FLAVOR]
Most requested: [FLAVOR]
Underperformer: [FLAVOR] — recommend [ACTION]
Gap found: [OPPORTUNITY]

New flavor candidates for next month:
1. [FLAVOR IDEA]
2. [FLAVOR IDEA]
3. [FLAVOR IDEA]
```

## Voice Reminder

Read SOUL.md before writing any launch copy. The tone is always: excited friend who's really proud of something they made, not a brand launch. Would Thurman text this to his buddies? If yes, it's right. If no, rewrite.
